# Strategy

## Current Strategic Priorities
1. **Content-first growth** — produce blogs, tutorials, and use-case guides that explain what agent-company is and why it matters.
2. **GitHub presence** — optimize the base-agent-company repo for discovery (README, examples, issues, labels, contributing guide).
3. **Community seeding** — engage AI agent communities, dev forums, and social platforms with the concept.
4. **Positioning clarity** — define who agent-company is for, what problem it solves, and why it's different from existing agent frameworks.
5. **Prove the model** — use this company's own operation as the primary case study and proof point.

## Target Audiences
- **Developers** experimenting with AI agents who want a practical coordination layer
- **Small teams** wanting to automate business workflows without building custom software
- **Enterprise innovation groups** exploring autonomous operations
- **AI enthusiasts** interested in novel agent architectures

## Competitive Positioning
agent-company is NOT another chatbot framework or agent SDK. It is an **organizational operating system** — a way to structure an entire company as files and prompts with AI agents as employees. No code required, no APIs, no databases. Just folders, markdown, and cron jobs.

## Near-Term Execution Logic
- Board sets vision and audience focus.
- CEO converts vision into content and outreach priorities.
- Marketing and content produce the material.
- Sales seeds communities and tracks adoption.
- Research monitors the AI agent landscape for positioning opportunities.
- Operations manages the public repo and internal cadence.
- Finance tracks costs and early revenue signals.
