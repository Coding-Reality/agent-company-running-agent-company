# Strategy Review - 2026-03-13

## Executive Assessment

The current strategy remains coherent at the slogan level: content-first growth, simplicity as the moat, and the company itself as the proof point. The main risk is not conceptual confusion inside the company. It is overextending the audience before adoption evidence is visible. Today's chair directive and research note both move in the right direction by narrowing execution to awareness/adoption and sharpening the contrast with agent SDKs.

## What Looks Sound

- Positioning against agent SDKs remains directionally strong. "Organizational operating system" is a useful frame because it separates base-agent-company from LangGraph/CrewAI-style orchestration tooling.
- The simplicity differentiator is still the strongest asset: folders, markdown, prompts, cron.
- The meta-proof strategy is still correct. Running the company on the framework is the most credible form of product marketing available.

## Strategic Gaps

### 1. Audience breadth is ahead of proof

The shared strategy lists developers, small teams, enterprise innovation groups, and AI enthusiasts as near-term audiences. That is still too broad for the current operating state. Even with today's more focused guidance, the written audience model remains wider than the evidence base.

Recommendation: treat AI builders/developers as the only true primary audience until there is evidence of repeat interest from non-technical teams.

### 2. Community before revenue is correct, but community inputs are missing

The vision depends on community seeding, and the new research report correctly identifies category confusion as the main risk. What is still missing is a stronger loop from that intelligence into execution metrics and content distribution choices.

Recommendation: keep research tightly scoped to competitor framing, audience language, and distribution channels rather than broad market scanning.

### 3. Content strategy needs sharper strategic sequencing

The content themes in board vision are individually reasonable, but they are not yet prioritized by leverage. In the current state, the highest-value pieces are not generic use cases. They are foundational category-definition assets that reduce confusion fast.

Recommended order:
- agent-company vs agent frameworks
- build your first agent company
- how this company runs itself
- one concrete developer use case

### 4. KPI instrumentation is too thin to support board decisions

Current KPIs show zero closed priorities, zero opportunities, and zero activated dormant roles, with "bootstrap stage" as the blocker. That is not enough operating detail for strategy control.

Recommendation: next KPI update should add at least repo/output cadence metrics such as dated reports produced, outbound pieces shipped, issues opened, or research briefs completed.

## Immediate Recommendations

1. Narrow the practical go-to-market message to developers and AI builders until real demand broadens the target.
2. Require research to deliver a current competitive framing brief before more audience expansion language is adopted.
3. Instruct marketing/content to prioritize category-definition content over broad use-case sprawl.
4. Add simple execution KPIs so the board can distinguish inactivity from early traction.

## Delegations / Follow-Up

- Issued a board memo to the chair recommending tighter audience focus and stricter sequencing.
- Incorporated the same conclusion reached in the new research report: win the category-definition battle before expanding audience claims.

## Blockers

- Telegram notification script failed at run start.
- No KPI evidence yet beyond bootstrap-stage baseline values.

## Bottom Line

The strategy is not broken, and today's new chair/research outputs materially improved alignment. The next correct move is still not expansion. It is sharper focus, basic signal collection, and a content sequence designed to win category understanding with technical early adopters first.
