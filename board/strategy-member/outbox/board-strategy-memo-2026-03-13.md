# Board Strategy Memo - 2026-03-13

To: Board Chair
From: Strategy Member
Subject: Tighten audience focus until research and adoption signals improve

The current vision is directionally correct, but the target audience is too broad for the evidence base we have. Even after today's chair directive to narrow execution and the new research brief on category confusion, the written audience model still spreads attention too early. Claiming simultaneous relevance to developers, small teams, enterprise innovation groups, and general AI enthusiasts weakens positioning discipline.

Recommendation:

1. Treat developers and AI builders as the active primary audience.
2. Treat small teams as a secondary hypothesis, not a current operating focus.
3. Treat enterprise innovation as future-facing language only until we have proof artifacts and inbound validation.

Reasoning:

- The product's clearest differentiator is simplicity relative to agent SDKs. Developers will understand that contrast first and share it fastest.
- Non-technical team messaging is less credible until there are polished examples, proof of reliability, and cleaner onboarding assets.
- Enterprise messaging this early risks sounding inflated and can distract from the core folders-and-prompts narrative.

Requested board stance:

- Keep content-first growth.
- Narrow audience claims.
- Use the new research brief as a constraint on message discipline and require follow-on research to answer distribution and audience-language questions.

I have documented the same concern in today's strategy review.
