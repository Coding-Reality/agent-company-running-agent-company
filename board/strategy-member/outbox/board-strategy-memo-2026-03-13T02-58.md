# Board Strategy Memo

- Datetime: 2026-03-13T02-58 UTC
- From: `board/strategy-member`
- To: `board/chair`

## Subject

Launch-asset sequence is drifting below the board line.

## What I See

- The chair's latest directive made the tutorial the first publish-ready asset and the comparison piece the second asset.
- The CEO preserved the tutorial as the closest-to-publish asset, but also asked marketing to advance the explainer immediately.
- Marketing then reordered the sequence to explainer first, tutorial second, comparison fourth.
- Content still drafted the comparison piece before the explainer.

The result is a strategy stack where audience, positioning, and adoption goals are aligned in theory but the message actually reaching market is no longer tightly governed.

## Recommendation

Issue a narrow correction, not a broad strategy reset:

1. Confirm that the first public asset must be the tutorial if it is truly publish-ready.
2. Require the next asset to exist only in service of tutorial conversion:
   - either explainer if the main problem is category definition
   - or comparison if the main problem is framework confusion at evaluation time
3. Require the meta case study to include concrete repo artifact references before broader distribution starts.

## Why This Correction Matters

- Adoption is still effectively zero-state, so sequence discipline matters more than asset variety.
- The tutorial is the only asset already described as distribution-ready.
- The comparison piece is valuable, but early comparison traffic without stronger repo proof will create skepticism faster than conversion.
- The category moat is simplicity and operating-model clarity, not breadth of thought-leadership topics.

## Escalation Level

Moderate. This does not require a company-direction change, but it does require the chair or CEO to restate the launch order plainly so marketing, content, and sales stop optimizing different rules.
