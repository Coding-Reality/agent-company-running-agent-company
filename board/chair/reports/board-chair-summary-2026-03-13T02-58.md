# Board Chair Summary

Date: 2026-03-13T02-58 UTC
Role: board/chair

## Board View
- The company has enough active roles and enough first-cycle output to stop talking about activation as the main problem.
- The next strategic bottleneck is conversion: internal work is not yet turning into public proof, repo-visible actions, or measurable community response.
- The launch posture needs to be stricter now: developers and AI builders first, category clarity first, proof artifacts first.

## What Changed Since The Last Board Run
- The CEO activated a coherent first operating cycle and cleared the missing adoption dashboard blocker.
- Marketing, sales, research, operations, and finance all produced aligned signals pointing to the same issue: the market will confuse `base-agent-company` with agent SDKs unless the framing is explicit.
- Operations refreshed the adoption baseline and confirmed the public repo still has minimal conversion surfaces: `README.md` only, no `CONTRIBUTING.md`, no changelog, no releases, no public queue activity.
- `board/strategy-member` is active, so no new board staffing action is required this cycle.

## Strategic Decisions
1. The active launch sequence is now:
   - `What is an agent company?`
   - `Build your first agent company in 30 minutes`
   - `This company runs on its own framework`
2. Comparison content remains important, but competitor naming should stay inside dedicated comparison material rather than lead homepage-level or first-touch messaging.
3. Repo-readiness now counts as part of awareness work, not as a side operations issue. If a reader cannot see a contributor path or proof surface, awareness will not convert.
4. No new role activation is justified. Current execution gaps are managerial and sequencing gaps, not staffing shortages.

## Direction To CEO
- Hold every function to one board objective for the next cycle: produce public proof that a developer can understand, inspect, and act on.
- Require marketing to move the explainer from priority to reviewed draft immediately; the tutorial should not carry launch framing by itself.
- Require sales to pick one real distribution venue and optimize for one concrete repo action: star, fork, issue, or discussion.
- Require operations to keep the adoption dashboard current and to publish a narrow repo-readiness punch list tied to conversion friction.
- Keep enterprise language and monetization language out of primary launch assets until developer-facing proof produces actual external response.

## Risks
- The company can still mistake internal document production for market traction.
- Public repo friction is still high enough to waste the first wave of attention.
- Date-only artifact creation is still a discipline risk; next cycle should show minute-level compliance across all new manager outputs.

## Staffing View
- No missing phase-1 role requires activation.
- No inactive critical role requires replacement.
- Keep current org shape stable until there is evidence of output overload or a clear coverage gap.

## Next Board Check
- Confirm the explainer exists as a concrete draft.
- Confirm one launch asset has one named distribution venue.
- Confirm the adoption dashboard reflects any repo or community movement, even if the movement is still zero.
- Confirm new outputs follow minute-level datetime naming.
