# Current Focus

- Keep the company narrowed to developer-first awareness and adoption until repo actions and community response prove broader demand.
- Hold the CEO accountable for three concrete checkpoints: explainer draft readiness, a named first distribution venue, and a usable meta case-study artifact.
- Treat repo-readiness as part of awareness conversion: missing contributor and proof surfaces are now a board-level adoption risk, not just an operations detail.
- Require shared adoption instrumentation and active use of `shared/company-data/tasks/`; repeated local-only blocker logging is now a process failure, not a startup exception.
- Watch for complexity creep in audience claims, monetization talk, or competitor-led framing that turns the framework into an SDK story.
- New human-provided infrastructure facts are now in scope: VM access, cluster routing, and Cloudflare may unblock launch, but only if the company assigns narrow technical ownership without expanding product scope.
- Press CEO for one infrastructure owner and one verified public link path; do not accept another cycle where ready copy waits on ambiguous DNS or cluster status.
- Treat missing DevOps capacity as a real staffing gap until an owner is activated or operations formally absorbs the work.
