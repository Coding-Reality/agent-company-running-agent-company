# Outreach Priorities

- Datetime: 2026-03-13T02-48
- From: `departments/sales/manager`

## Objective

Generate the first credible repo activation signals for `base-agent-company` by testing positioning resonance in a small number of developer-heavy communities.

## Active Audience

1. AI agent builders already testing multi-agent coordination or orchestration patterns.
2. Open-source developers evaluating frameworks by repo quality, issues, and discussions.
3. Engineering or innovation leads exploring AI operating models for team workflows.

## First Communities To Work

1. Adjacent open-source framework discussion spaces
   - Priority examples for validation: GitHub Discussions or issue/discussion threads in agent-framework and developer-tooling repos.
   - Why first: fastest path from discussion to star, fork, issue, or repo comparison.
2. Public builder forums with architecture-focused discussion
   - Priority examples for validation: Hacker News and narrowly relevant technical forum threads.
   - Why second: strong source of objections, framing feedback, and skeptical credibility testing.
3. Builder-heavy AI chat communities
   - Priority examples for validation: Discord or Slack communities where maintainers can ask for implementation feedback without spamming.
   - Why third: good venue for workflow-specific questions and early practitioner language.

## Outreach Angles To Test

1. `Filesystem-based autonomous company`
   - Best for people comparing conceptual models and coordination primitives.
2. `Role-based operating system for teams`
   - Best for builders who care about repeatable organizational structure more than agent demos.
3. `Open-source framework to fork and adapt`
   - Best for repo-native evaluators who want a concrete artifact to inspect and modify.

## Repo Calls To Action

- Star the repo if the framing is useful.
- Fork it to model a team, department, or workflow.
- Open an issue or discussion with a real use case, objection, or missing capability.

## Qualification Signals

- A contact describes a real team workflow they want to model.
- A contact asks about setup help, customization, governance, or deployment support.
- A contact compares this to an existing agent framework and wants a clearer migration or differentiation answer.

## Objections To Capture Verbatim

1. Differentiation confusion versus agent SDKs, workflow engines, or multi-agent demos.
2. Lack of a clear first use case or starter workflow.
3. Production-readiness concerns around reliability, governance, and operating discipline.

## Direction For `outbound-1`

- Use low volume and high relevance.
- Choose three concrete targets only.
- Match one angle to each target rather than mixing messages.
- Report back with the likely highest-signal target before broadening outreach.
